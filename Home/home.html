Analysis of human and mice subjects with Rett syndrome

Rett syndrome is a rare neurological genetic disorder that affects the development of the brain, resulting in severe physical and intellectual disabilities. It affects children (especially females) in the early years of life, after a period of apparent normality, and is caused by mutations in the MeCP2 gene (Ethel cpg-binding protein 2) located in the X chromosome.
Symptoms of Rett syndrome can typically include loss of verbal and motor skills, repetitive hand movements, breathing problems, seizures, and scoliosis; the severity of symptoms can vary considerably and symptoms similar to those of the diagnostic category of autism spectrum disorders (ASD) begin to manifest.
Currently there is no cure for this disease; however, there are treatments available to manage the symptoms and improve the quality of life of people with Rett syndrome. In most cases, the diagnosis is confirmed by molecular genetic testing, thus avoiding a misdiagnosis of autism, as was frequently the case in the past.

In recent years a lot of data has been collected, following studies based on human and mouse tests; raw input data of these studies are freely available on public archives, such as Sequence Read Archive (SRA), managed by the National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI) .
These data represent a wealth of information for further global and integrative retrospective analysis. To identify applied biological processes and reveal possible genes responsible for this genetic mutation, we have created a Database Overview section where you can load your own set of transcriptomic data with RNA sequences of subjects with Rett syndrome subjected to various tests and compare the results with better visualization.
Since each of the laboratories producing these data sets has analyzed their data following different protocols (e.g. in terms of software, genomic versions and many other features), the data has been arranged in this web portal to allow easy viewing, comparison, interrogation and download. Rett Syndrome Portal is a free web server, able to interrogate differentially expressed genes (Degs), using different criteria, providing new information on the transcription landscape of individuals with Rett syndrome.
